hessi_flare HESSI Flare List

Purpose:
Description:

The RHESSI flare list is created during quicklook processing (and is often re-processed).



The flare list is obtained by comparing the observing summary count rate in the 6 to 12 keV energy band to a

threshold. The count rate threshold is determined from the background level; which is obtained using a 60 second

running average. For an interval to be considered as a flare candidate, the count rate must be greater than 3*sigma

above the background level. Variations in the count rate due to changes in attenuator state or decimation state are

taken into account by use of the "corrected" count rate method, which empirically determines the corrections.



A flare candidate is flagged as a possible solar flare if the ratio of the count rate in the front detectors to total count

rate is 3 sigma above its own background level (also determined using a 60 second running average). An exception

is made if there is an SAA crossing or data gap; for those cases the front - total ratio must be greater than 60% in the

interval for that interval to be considered to be a possible flare.



Even with the front - total ratio test, particle precipitation events can be confused with flares. The candidate is only

confirmed as a solar flare if a valid position is found in the 6 to 12 keV energy band.
Parameters

hef_id HEC id

Event identification number (HEC internal number)

time_start UT

Start time determined from when the flux rate in the 6-12 keV band exceeds 3*sigma above the background

level calcualted on a 60s running average.

time_peak UT

The time of the peak flux for the flare in the energy range greater than 6 keV

time_end UT

End time determined from when the flux rate in the 6-12 keV band drops below 3*sigma of the background

level calcualted on a 60s running average.

nar NAR #

The number for the closest NOAA active region index

x_arcsec arcsec

Position from centre of the sun.

y_arcsec arcsec

Position from centre of the sun.

radial_arcsec arcsec

Radial distance from the centre of the Sun.

duration s

Duration of the flare in seconds

count_sec_peak Unitless

Peak counts/second in energy range 12-25 keV, averaged over active collimators, including background.

total_count Unitless

Total counts in energy range 12-25 keV integrated over duration of flare summed over all subcollimators,

including background.

energy_kev keV

The highest energy band in which the flare was observed.
