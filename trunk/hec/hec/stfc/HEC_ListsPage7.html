proton_flux pfu

Proton fluxes are integral 5-minute averages for energies > 10 MeV, given in Particle Flux Units (pfu),

measured by GOES spacecraft at Geosynchronous orbit: 1 pfu = 1 p/sq. cm-s-sr.

Different detectors, onboard various GOES spacecraft, have taken the data since 1976. These proton data were

processed using various algorithms. To date, no attempt has been made to cross-normalize the resulting proton fluxes.

bas_magnetic_storms BAS Magnetic Storms

Purpose:
List of geo-effective magnetic storms (Contibuted by R Horne, BAS)
Description:

This list has been contributed by R Horne from the British Antarctic Survey. Storms are identified as a variation in

the terrestrial ring current characterised by an excursion of Dst below -20nT with a minimum below -50nT.
Parameters

bms_id HEC id

Event identification number (HEC internal number)

time_start UT

Start time of a geo-effective storm determined by a drop in the Dst.

time_peak UT

The time of maximum enhancement of the ring-current during a geo-effective storm as characterised by the

maximum (negative) deviation of the Dst index.

time_end UT

End associated with the end of a geo-effective storm as characterised by the dst index returning to its

nominal value.
