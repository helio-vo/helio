HELIO HEC Lists
goes_xray_flare GOES GEV Event List

Purpose:
List of X-ray flares based on observations by the GOES spacecraft
Description:

List generated by NOAA-SWPC based on observations in 1-8 Å from the XRS instrument on the GOES spacecraft.



There are usually two GOES spececraft operational resulting in virtually complete coverage since the 1970s. Recent

segments of the catalogue can be found through the NOAA site; archive segments held by the NGDC.
Parameters

goes_id HEC id

Event identification number (HEC internal number)

ntime_start UT

Adjusted start time of flare - new start time = old start time - 2x dt between start and peak

time_start UT

Start time of flare

time_peak UT

Peak time of flare

time_end UT

End time of flare

ntime_end UT

Adjusted end time of flare - new start time = old end time - 7x dt between end and peak

nar NAR #

NOAA active region index

latitude degrees

Heliographic latitude of event

longitude degrees

Stonyhurst longitude of active region

long_carr degrees

Carrington longitude of active region

xray_class X-ray class

Importance of flare at X-ray wavelengths -- the peak flux measured at Earth in the 0.1 to 0.8 nm range in

units of Watts/sq meter. The letters C, M and X stand for powers of 10: C denotes the power 10 to the -6, M

the power 10 to the -5, and X any power greater than 10 to the -4. Digits from 1.0 to 9.9 following C, M, and X

act as multipliers. For example, C3.2 stands for an X-ray burst with a maximum flux of 3.2 x 10E-5 Watts/sq

meter.
